Who We Are

Community Needs Assessment Overview

This assessment investigated the needs, interests, preferences, and opinions of Brookline residents aged 60 or older. Conducted by the Center for Social & Demographic Research on Aging (CSDRA) at UMass Boston on behalf of the Town of Brookline and Council on Aging, these findings align municipal planning with the reported experiences of older residents.

Reported Transportation Barriers & Gaps:

  • Medical transportation: Focus group participants repeatedly identified medical transportation as a core service supporting independence, expressing concern about reductions in service.
  • Transit limitations: Subsidized rideshare and medical transport rides remain limited, particularly for residents who do not qualify for regional adaptive transit.
  • Infrastructure concerns: Residents cited unsafe sidewalks, winter ice conditions, and pedestrian crossing gaps as key mobility obstacles.
  • Accessibility barriers: 39% of residents age 75+ report a disability; stakeholders noted that many homes, businesses, and public spaces remain difficult to navigate with walkers or wheelchairs.
"Getting around if unable to drive as we age. lack of cheaper transport; I don't use the T anymore as it's so jerky and I'm afraid I'll fall; also hard to get into. There are not enough subsidized Lyft rides per month, and not enough medical transport rides." — Brookline survey respondent

Strategic Recommendations

Key Recommendations From the Report

Note: The strategic recommendations listed below are quoted directly from the official Community Needs Assessment report produced by the UMass Boston Gerontology Institute for the Town of Brookline.

Financial Security & Tax Relief

Address financial security by continuing to review and expand existing property tax relief programs to help older homeowners remain in their homes.

Outreach & In-Home Supports

Prioritize outreach and in-home supports for adults ages 80 and older, particularly those living alone or without nearby family support.

Caregiver Support

Expand support for family caregivers and identify pathways to reach "younger older adults" who would benefit from caregiving support.

Medical Transportation

Prioritize the restoration and expansion of transportation services that help older adults access healthcare appointments.

Accessibility Review

Conduct an accessibility review of municipal facilities, community destinations, and public spaces, and continue to incorporate Age-Friendly principles across municipal planning.

Long-Term Facilities Strategy

Develop a long-term facilities strategy that enables more residents to be served by the Senior Center, including consideration of renovation, expansion, or satellite service delivery models.

Coordinated Communications

Reposition the Senior Center as a central hub for information, engagement, and community connection by leading or participating in a coordinated communications strategy using multiple platforms.

"I'm sure you all are doing excellent work but for folks like me who do not need or access your offerings you need to publicize your programs so that they reach folks like me." — Brookline survey respondent
