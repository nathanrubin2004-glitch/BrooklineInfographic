Strategic Recommendations

Strategic Recommendations from the Executive Summary

Executive Summary Attribution: The following strategic recommendations are reproduced directly from the official Community Needs Assessment report (produced by the UMass Boston Gerontology Institute for the Town of Brookline) and do not represent new recommendations created by this infographic.

Financial Security & Tax Relief

Address financial security by continuing to review and expand existing property tax relief programs to help older homeowners remain in their homes.

Outreach & In-Home Supports

Prioritize outreach and in-home supports for adults ages 80 and older, particularly those living alone or without nearby family support.

Caregiver Support

Expand support for family caregivers and identify pathways to reach "younger older adults" who would benefit from caregiving support.

Medical Transportation

Prioritize the restoration and expansion of transportation services that help older adults access healthcare appointments.

Accessibility Review

Conduct an accessibility review of municipal facilities, community destinations, and public spaces, and continue to incorporate Age-Friendly principles across municipal planning.

Long-Term Facilities Strategy

Develop a long-term facilities strategy that enables more residents to be served by the Senior Center, including consideration of renovation, expansion, or satellite service delivery models.

Coordinated Communications

Reposition the Senior Center as a central hub for information, engagement, and community connection by leading or participating in a coordinated communications strategy using multiple platforms.

"I'm sure you all are doing excellent work but for folks like me who do not need or access your offerings you need to publicize your programs so that they reach folks like me." — Brookline survey respondent
